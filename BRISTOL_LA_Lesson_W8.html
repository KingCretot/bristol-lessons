🔮 Predicting

Making Predictions

A prediction is a smart guess about what will happen next. Good readers use clues from the story and what they already know. 🔍

📖 Read this:

Bramble the bear cub looked up at the buzzing beehive high in the tree. He licked his lips. Slowly, he reached for the lowest branch and began to climb...

🧩 How to Predict

Clue: Bramble licked his lips and climbed toward the hive.
What I know: Bears love honey.
My prediction: Bramble is going to try to reach the honey!

❓ Ask & Answer

Questions About a Story

Strong readers ask questions and find answers right in the text. Question words help: who, what, where, when, why, how. 🧐

❓ The Question Words

Whoasks about a person or animal
Whereasks about a place
Whenasks about a time
Whyasks for a reason

On Saturday, Owen the owl flew to the meadow because he wanted to find a juicy worm for his dinner.

👣 Plural Nouns

More Than One

A plural noun means MORE THAN ONE. Most nouns add -s. But after s, x, ch, sh, or z, we add -es. 🔢

👣 Making Plurals

Add -sfox cub → cubs, tree → trees
Add -esbush → bushes, fox → foxes
When -es?after s, x, ch, sh, z
Tricky onesmouse → mice, child → children

🔄 Word Pairs

Synonyms & Antonyms

A synonym is a word that means the SAME. An antonym is a word that means the OPPOSITE. 🔄

🔄 Same or Opposite?

Synonymsbig = large  ·  happy = glad
Antonymshot ↔ cold  ·  up ↔ down
Memory trickS-ynonym = S-ame
