🧑 Characters & Setting

Who and Where

Every story has characters (the people or animals) and a setting (where and when it happens). 🏞️

🔢 Retell & Sequence

Telling the Story In Order

When we retell a story, we tell the events in order. Order words help: first, next, then, last. ⏭️

🗺️ Order Words

FirstThe event that starts it all
Next / ThenWhat happens in the middle
Last / FinallyHow the story ends
Why?Order helps the reader follow along

🏷️ Nouns

Common & Proper Nouns

A noun is a person, place, animal, or thing. A proper noun names a SPECIFIC one — and it always starts with a capital letter! 🔠

🏷️ Two Kinds of Nouns

Common nounany one of a kind: dog, city, girl
Proper nouna special name: Rex, Arcadia, Bristol
Capital ruleProper nouns ALWAYS get a capital
Examplesfox → Finn   ·   month → March

🏃 Verbs

Action Words & Past Tense

A verb is an action word — it tells what someone DOES. When the action already happened, we use past tense (often by adding -ed). 🏃

🏃 Verbs in Action

Finn runs. Pip hops. The owl flies. The bear sleeps.

Now (present)jump, play, walk
Already happened (past)jumped, played, walked
The trickMany verbs add -ed for past tense
Watch outSome change fully: run → ran, go → went

✏️ Commas

Commas in Lists & Dates

A comma is a tiny pause. We use commas to separate items in a list and to separate the day from the year in a date. ✏️

✏️ Two Comma Jobs

In a listFinn packed a scarf, a snack, and a map.
In a dateJuly 4, 2026
List ruleA comma after every item except the last
Date ruleA comma goes between the day and the year
